# Handshake — Pitch Guide (Kenyan Schools Market)

## The Product

AI-powered matchmaking for events. Every attendee gets introduced to 3-5 people they should meet — based on goals, skills, and what they're building. No awkward networking. No fishing for conversations.

---

## Target Customer

Primary: Schools & universities (graduation dinners, career fairs, hackathons)
Secondary: Corporate events, conferences, hackathon organizers

---

## Pitch Angle

> "Your students spent 4 years together but only know a handful of people. Handshake makes sure every graduate leaves with meaningful connections before they scatter into the world."

Position as a **career services / student affairs tool**, not entertainment:

- Career services is measured on outcomes (job placements, alumni network strength)
- This is a career outcomes tool disguised as a dinner icebreaker
- Give them a metric: "80% of graduates made a connection that could lead to collaboration"

---

## Pricing (KES)

### Per-event (paid by institution)

| Tier | What they get | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Pilot (single event) | One graduation dinner / career fair, any size | KES 25,000-35,000 |
| Annual | All graduation events for the year | KES 300,000 |
| Campus license | Unlimited events, career fairs, hackathons | KES 600,000 |

### Alternative models

- **Sponsor model**: Free for the school. A brand (Safaricom, Equity, etc.) pays KES 150,000-300,000 to be mentioned in every intro message.
- **Student council budget**: KES 15,000-25,000 from discretionary event budget — easier to approve.
- **Department pilot**: Pitch a single department (CS, Engineering, Business) for KES 15,000-20,000.

### Why KES 25,000-35,000 is the sweet spot for a pilot

- A student council or department can approve this without multi-level finance approval
- Comparable to 2-3 decent dinner tables — small enough to be impulsive
- Frame as a **student-led initiative**, not a vendor pitch: "My team built this, we want to pilot with our own graduation."

---

## How It Works (For the Organizer)

1. **Setup** — Organizer sends a form link or Telegram bot code to attendees
2. **Registration** — Students fill a 5-minute form (name, role, goals, what they're building)
3. **Matching** — AI creates introductions based on complementary goals and skills
4. **Event** — During the dinner, students get Telegram messages: "Go find Sarah at table 7 — you both want to build in climate tech"
5. **Follow-up** — After the event, each person gets the other's contact info. Connection is recorded on-chain (optional).

---

## Prompts the Organizer Can Curate

Give the organizer a simple form to customize questions:

**Career-oriented (for graduation dinner):**
- What industry are you heading into?
- What's one skill you want to build in the next year?
- What kind of people do you want in your network?
- What's a project you're proud of from school?
- Where do you see yourself in 3 years?

**Hackathon-oriented:**
- What's your tech stack?
- What problem do you want to solve?
- Looking for a co-founder? Designer? Developer?

---

## Raw Script (For Walking Into the HOD's Office)

> "Hey [HOD/Council Chair], I built a networking bot for our graduation dinner. Every student gets matched with 3-5 people they should actually meet — based on their goals, skills, and what they're building. I want to run it for our graduation. It costs KES 30,000 to cover infrastructure. That's KES 60 per student if we have 500 people. I'll handle everything — just give me 10 minutes in the program."

**If they say no:**
- "What budget do you have for student activities this event?"
- "Would you be open to a free pilot and we talk about payment if it works?"

## The Escalation Plan

| Phase | Target | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Pilot at your own school (1-2 events) | KES 25,000-35,000 or free |
| Phase 2 | Use data + testimonials to sell to 3-5 schools | KES 75,000-150,000 |
| Phase 3 | Build pipeline of regular corporate events | KES 150,000-300,000 |
| Phase 4 | Sponsor model — onboard 1-2 telcos/banks | Scale |

---

## Metrics That Sell (Track These)

- Number of connections made per event
- % of attendees who got at least one match
- Post-event survey: "Did this improve your experience?"
- Alumni network growth (connections formed that outlast the event)

---

## Competition / Alternatives

| Alternative | Why Handshake wins |
|---|---|
| Random name-picking / icebreaker games | No signal — random connections waste time |
| Speed networking (everyone rotates) | Forced, exhausting, low depth |
| LinkedIn / Bumble Bizz | No curation, no event context, no AI negotiation |
| Do nothing | Students leave with 2-3 connections they walked in with |

---

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Why should we pay for this?" | "Your alumni network is your biggest asset. Most graduates leave knowing 5 people. This doubles or triples that. You're not buying software — you're buying a better alumni network starting night one." |
| "Students won't use it." | "They register in 5 minutes on their phone. We've run it at MiniHack Kenya — engagement was high." |
| "KES 30,000 is too much for a pilot." | "That's KES 60 a head for 500 students. Compare that to a photo booth or a guest speaker." |
| "We already do icebreakers." | "Icebreakers are random. This is intentional — AI finds the people they'd actually benefit from meeting." |
